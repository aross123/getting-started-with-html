How the internet works

The WWW (world wide web) is held together entirely by hyperlinks. Hyperlinks are what quite literally make the internet a web, linking one website to another.

Computers use HTTP to communicate with browsers, that then send the requests over the internet to servers.These servers then send the requested documents back to our computers

HTTP: Hypertext transfer protocol. How data communicates through The Web.

HTML: HyperText Markup Language. Language used to build websites.

Computers are not stupid.
They just take things a little too literally sometimes.

How to create browsers

HTML is the basis for everything. There are three essential parts of HTML.

Text content: What the page is actually going to end up looking like.

Markup: Tags, or what you use to to make the content presentable.
Example: b=bold, em=italicized, a=anchor, br=break, p=paragraph.

Refrences to other documents: Attributes are what allow you to make links.
[Tag ATTR=“value”> contents [/tag>
